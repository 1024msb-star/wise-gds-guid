Installation Pattern Overview

Three Common Installation Patterns

P810 is a common option for local pressure indication. However, when an indicator type pressure switch provides local indication, a separate gauge may be omitted depending on the customer specification. The transmitter and the switch do not replace each other; they serve different purposes.

Transmitter and Switch Serve Different Purposes
UPT863 (Software-Based Monitoring)
PLC/HMI receives the 4–20 mA signal and monitors continuous pressure values and trends. Interlock behavior depends on the PLC, wiring, and control logic design.
P840 Indicator Pressure Switch
Provides local pressure indication and a separate contact signal when the set pressure is reached. Actual shut-off behavior depends on the site interlock design, such as safety relay, ESD circuit, or PLC.
In safety-critical hazardous-gas lines, a transmitter for continuous PLC/HMI monitoring is often used together with an indicator pressure switch that provides local indication and a separate contact signal. In this case, a separate P810 gauge is optional depending on the customer P&ID.

Stock Mix Rationale

Separate the pressure-range mix from the product-family mix. The product-family mix may vary depending on each customer’s installation pattern distribution.

Pressure Range Mix — Initial Stock Assumption
0–3,000 psi — Initial assumption: approx. 20%
Mainly for the high-pressure side of gas cabinets. Demand depends heavily on hazardous-gas applications.
0–160 psi — Initial assumption: approx. 80%
Low-pressure distribution section of VMB/VMP. Most gas-using tools pass through this section, but the instrument combination varies by gas type and customer standard.
Initial Stock Strategy
20/80 is an initial assumption
For initial stock planning, P810 can be considered first because it has the lowest entry barrier. Later, adding UPT863 and P840 to stock can help respond faster to customers’ short-lead-time requirements.
